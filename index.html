Improving your Remote Lifestyle and Learning during the Pandemic

Description
With a link!

  • Even when classes are in-person, students often have difficulties with time management, especially those who work, have families, or have other responsibilities.
    With classes being remote, time management becomes even more difficult, but there are still many things you can do to ease your stress and use your time better.

  • Creating a schedule

    Google calendar, Tracking class/assignment schedule

  • Invest in a planner

    List assignments, due dates, goals, etc.

  • Turn off your phone

    It is easy to waste time on your phone if it is available, so try turning it off and see how much more productive you are


  • Many students experience burnout because they are overloaded and do not take the time to reward themselves.
    While it is important to complete assignments and spend time learning, it is equally important to reward your hard work and give yourself something to look forward to.

  • Reward yourself

    This could be as simple as taking a break, playing video games, watching TV, or buying yourself something

  • Manage Your Sleep Schedule

    Find a schedule that fits your classes and works with you
    Try to get 8 hours of sleep
    Your phone comes with a sleep manager, a "night mode" to make the light on it less harsh, and do not disturb mode if you find yourself waking up because of notifications.

  • Make Time For Physical Activity

    If you normally walk to class, try taking a walk before class to get energy and simulate a change
    If you enjoy working out, do that, otherwise at least get up and take 250 steps every hour (or set your own goal)

  • Creating a Workspace

    Doing your work in a different place from where you sleep/relax
    Working in an environment without distractions
    Don’t join zoom calls or do homework from bed

  • Do Something to Get Ready

    If you don’t have to leave the house, it is easy to neglect getting ready, so take time to shower, get dressed, pretend we don’t live in a post-apocalyptic hell
